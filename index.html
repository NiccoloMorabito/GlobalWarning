Global Warning

A Look at the Impact of Greenhouse Gas Emissions on Climate

The Problem

Climate change is a global phenomenon caused by the increase of greenhouse gases (GHGs) in the atmosphere. These gases, including carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), and F-gases (hydrofluorocarbons and perfluorocarbons), trap heat from the sun and warm the planet, leading to a warming of the Earth's climate and general changes in weather patterns and ecosystems.

How are overall global GHG emissions evolving over time?

All the gases use the same unit of measurement (MtCO2) that represents the number of metric tons of a GHG whose atmospheric impact has been standardized to that of one unit mass of carbon dioxide, based on the global warming potential of the gas. This is the reason why it makes sense to directly compare the different gases.

The Consequences

The consequences of the greenhouse effect can include significant changes in global temperatures, sea level rise, altered precipitation patterns and extreme weather events (storms, hurricanes, heatwaves, droughts, etc). These changes can have severe impacts on ecosystems, human health and well-being, agriculture and the economy.

The Link

After noticing a similar behaviour between the increase of greenhouse gases emissions and the increase in the frequency of natural disasters in the last decades, it is crucial to study and analyze the two phenomena together in a comprehensive manner to study what is the actual relationship and validate the initial hypothesises, but also to deeply understand the data and make distinctions according to the time and the geographical area.

What is the relationship between the consequences of climate change and the emissions of GHG in different countries in the last century? Are the consequences homogeneously and/or fairly distributed in the world according to the GHG emissions? Does climate change affect different countries differently?
